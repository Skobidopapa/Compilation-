Paw-Ever In My Heart

Kuya Jigger

    It feels surreal writing this, a year having passed since that heartbreaking July day in 2024. The house feels… quieter. Emptier. There's a space, a palpable absence that no amount of time seems to fill. You were more than just a dog; you were family, our first furry child since 2016, a constant source of joy, laughter, and unwavering loyalty.
    I remember the day we brought you home. A tiny ball of fluff, so small you could fit in my hands. Even then, there was a spark in your eyes, a mischievous glint that hinted at the playful, spirited dog you would become. You quickly became the center of our world, a whirlwind of boundless energy and goofy charm. The countless games of fetch in the park, your ecstatic barks at the sight of squirrels, the way you'd snuggle up close on cold nights – these are memories I hold dear, treasures I revisit often.
    Your protective instincts were remarkable. You were our vigilant guardian, always alert, always ready to defend us, even from the most harmless of threats. That unwavering loyalty, that deep-seated love, was a constant comfort. You were our shadow, our confidante, our furry best friend. You understood us in ways that words couldn't express, offering silent companionship when we needed it most. Your presence was a constant source of comfort and security.
    Then came the illness, the slow decline that chipped away at your vibrant spirit. Watching you weaken, watching that playful spark dim, was agony. Each day was a battle, a fight against a cruel fate that we couldn't win. The pain of losing you is a wound that time has barely begun to heal. It's a constant ache, a reminder of the love we shared, the bond that death couldn't break.
    But even in this grief, there's solace. Solace in the memories, in the laughter, in the unconditional love you gave so freely. Solace in knowing that you lived a life full of love, surrounded by those who cherished you deeply. You may be gone, Kuya Jigger, but you are far from forgotten. You'll forever hold a special place in our hearts, a paw-sitive imprint on our lives that time can never erase. You are, and always will be, paw-ever in our hearts.
